The LinkedIn Glow-Up

Transform a "meh" profile into a recruiter magnet. Live makeover.

This session focuses on optimizing your LinkedIn profile to powerfully showcase your expertise and attract opportunities, making it a true digital representation of your **authority**.

Speech Outline (10 Minutes - AIDA Model):
  • Attention (1.5 min): Hook with the relatable problem of an overlooked LinkedIn profile.
    • "Is your LinkedIn a beacon of brilliance, or a digital tumbleweed? Many miss opportunities because their profile doesn't shine."
  • Interest (3 min): Explain the power of an optimized LinkedIn and its key components.
    • "It's more than a job board; it's your 24/7 spokesperson."
    • Briefly explain: compelling headlines, engaging "About" sections, quantifying achievements in experience, strategic use of skills & endorsements, and the power of recommendations.
  • Desire (3 min): Paint a picture of success and opportunity.
    • "Imagine recruiters actively seeking *you*, collaborators reaching out for *your expertise*."
    • Share an anonymized "before/after" impact example: "Think of Sarah, who shifted her headline from 'Marketing Coordinator' to 'Brand Storyteller & Digital Growth Strategist.' Within weeks, she saw a 300% increase in profile views."
  • Action (2.5 min): Provide immediate, actionable steps.
    • **Headline Audit:** "Craft 3 new headlines that scream your unique value."
    • **About Section Hook:** "Refine the first two lines to be captivating."
    • **Impactful Experience:** "Replace passive verbs with action verbs, add quantifiable results."
    • **Call to Action:** "Your LinkedIn is your digital **authority**. Dedicate 15 minutes this week to applying these fixes. Your future self will thank you!"
5-Minute Activity: Profile Power Scan
  • **Objective:** Participants quickly identify one immediate area for improvement on their own LinkedIn profile.
  • **How:** Speaker gives 2-3 rapid-fire prompts (e.g., "Does your headline clearly state your value?," "Is your 'About' section compelling?").
  • **Engagement:** Participants share their "aha!" moment or planned change in the chat.

Power Pose Rhetoric

Body language that screams "I run things." Strike a pose, get feedback.

Discover how intentional body language can dramatically enhance your confidence and perceived **authority**, particularly in virtual interactions, allowing you to command presence even through a screen.

Speech Outline (10 Minutes - AIDA Model):
  • Attention (1.5 min): Hook with the unspoken language of presence.
    • "Have you ever seen someone command attention without saying a word? It’s their **body language**. Are *your* non-verbal cues affirming your **authority**?"
    • Introduce the concept of "Power Pose Rhetoric" – silent communication that screams, 'I run things!'
  • Interest (3 min): Explain the science behind power posing and virtual adaptations.
    • Harvard's Amy Cuddy's research: how expansive poses can change physiology (lower cortisol, increase testosterone).
    • Focus on subtle yet powerful adjustments for virtual settings: open shoulders, chest, direct eye contact with the camera, grounded posture, calm breathing.
  • Desire (3 min): Envision commanding a room with silent communication.
    • "Imagine delivering a presentation where every gesture reinforces your message, inspiring trust and respect."
    • Real-world impact: differentiation in job interviews, client pitches, and team meetings. It signals competence, trustworthiness, and natural **authority**.
  • Action (2.5 min): Demonstrate and prompt practice of virtual power poses.
    • **The "Wonder Woman/Superman" (adapted seated):** Sit up straight, shoulders back, chest slightly open.
    • **The "Grounded Lean":** Lean slightly forward when making a key point, signaling engagement.
    • **The "Open Embrace":** Keep arms uncrossed, hands visible, projecting openness when listening.
    • **Call to Action:** "Identify one meeting this week where you consciously adopt a power pose. Notice how you feel and how others respond. Your silent **rhetoric** can be your most powerful tool!"
5-Minute Activity: Virtual Confidence Stance
  • **Objective:** Participants silently practice one of the demonstrated power poses adapted for a virtual setting.
  • **How:** Speaker demonstrates 2-3 simple power poses. Participants are encouraged to turn on cameras (if comfortable) and practice.
  • **Engagement:** Participants share one word in the chat describing how the pose made them feel.

The Elevator Pitch Seduction

A 30-second spiel that lands deals (or dates). Speed-dating style drills.

Learn to craft a concise, compelling, and memorable pitch that instantly communicates your value and **authority**, seizing every unexpected opportunity with confidence.

Speech Outline (10 Minutes - AIDA Model):
  • Attention (1.5 min): Hook with the missed opportunity of a weak introduction.
    • "You step into an elevator, beside you, the CEO of your dream company. You have 30 seconds. What do you say? Do you stumble, or do you captivate?"
    • Introduce "The Elevator Pitch Seduction" – a 30-second spiel that can land deals, collaborations, or even a date with opportunity.
  • Interest (3 min): Explain the broad utility of an elevator pitch and its components.
    • "The 'elevator pitch' isn't just for elevators; it's for networking, bios, impromptu meetings."
    • The **Three Pillars of an Irresistible Pitch:** 1) **Problem:** What common problem do you solve? 2) **Solution:** How do you solve it uniquely? 3) **Impact/Benefit:** What's the tangible result for them?
    • Why 30 seconds? It forces clarity, brevity, and impact, showcasing your professional **authority**.
  • Desire (3 min): Visualize confident, intriguing introductions.
    • "Imagine confidently introducing yourself, seeing heads nod, and hearing, 'Tell me more!'"
    • Impact on career/networking: It's about connecting, articulating your **authority** so clearly that people *want* to know more.
    • Example: Contrast a "graphic designer" pitch with "I help startups translate their innovative ideas into stunning visual brands that captivate their target audience and drive growth."
  • Action (2.5 min): Guide participants to build their core pitch.
    • **Identify Your Problem:** "What specific problem do you passionately solve for others?"
    • **State Your Unique Solution:** "How do *you* uniquely solve it? What's your secret sauce?"
    • **Quantify the Benefit:** "What tangible positive impact do you create?"
    • **Call to Action:** "Don't wait for the next CEO to appear. Write down your pitch, practice it daily, and be ready to seduce opportunity. Deliver your polished pitch to at least three people this week for feedback."
5-Minute Activity: Rapid-Fire Pitch Practice
  • **Objective:** Participants deliver their current or a quick draft of an elevator pitch and receive immediate, one-sentence feedback.
  • **How:** The host quickly sets up small breakout rooms (2-3 people). Each person has 60 seconds to deliver.
  • **Engagement:** Rejoin the main room for a quick debrief.

Brand or Bland?

Judge anonymous member bios—would you hire them? Brutal but fun.

Develop a keen eye for what makes a personal brand truly shine, ensuring yours is distinct, authentic, and radiates undeniable **authority** in a competitive landscape.

Speech Outline (10 Minutes - AIDA Model):
  • Attention (1.5 min): Hook with a comparison to famous brands.
    • "Think of your favorite brand – Nike, Apple. What comes to mind? Now, think about *your* brand. Are you memorable, impactful, essential? Or... just **bland**?"
    • Introduce "Brand or Bland?" – ensuring you radiate 'promotion material' energy.
  • Interest (3 min): Define personal branding and highlight the cost of blandness.
    • "Personal branding is the sum total of how others perceive you – your reputation, your unique value, your promise."
    • The cost of blandness: missed opportunities, less recognition, blending into the background.
    • **Key Elements of a Powerful Brand:** Authenticity, Clarity, Consistency, Value Proposition, Visibility.
  • Desire (3 min): Envision being the "hire me!" candidate.
    • "Imagine someone reading your bio or seeing your online presence and thinking, 'Yes! *That's* the person we need!'"
    • Case study: An expert consistently sharing content, speaking, and having a clear message attracts opportunities because their **authority** is undeniable.
    • Embrace your uniqueness; this is where true **authority** lies.
  • Action (2.5 min): Guide a personal brand audit.
    • **Three Words Test:** "What three words do you *want* people to use to describe you professionally?"
    • **Online Consistency Check:** "Is your message consistent across LinkedIn, bios, and introductions?"
    • **Seek Feedback:** "Ask a trusted colleague: 'When you think of my professional capabilities, what's the first thing that comes to mind?'"
    • **Call to Action:** "Your personal brand is your most valuable asset. Begin your brand audit today. Infuse it with your unique **authority** and spark. Remember, you'll radiate 'promotion material' energy – no suit required!"
5-Minute Activity: Spot the Spark
  • **Objective:** Participants analyze anonymized example bios and identify elements of strong personal branding/authority.
  • **How:** Host/speaker displays 2-3 anonymized bios (pre-collected). Participants have 60 seconds to read.
  • **Engagement:** A quick poll (or chat response) asks: "Which bio exudes the most 'Authority' and why?" Brief discussion in chat.
